Uses and Disclosures of PHI

BeAware uses or discloses PHI solely to perform services for Covered Entities under their service agreements, as permitted or required by the applicable BAA and HIPAA regulations.

Examples:

  • Permitted use: Translating speech to text in a clinical setting under contract with a healthcare provider.
  • De-identified data: Used to improve services only with express written consent, which can be revoked.

We may disclose PHI:

  • As required by law or valid subpoena,
  • To subcontractors/agents providing services to us that are bound by the same HIPAA obligations, or
  • Only with your express written consent in any other scenario.

Your Rights (Residents of U.S.)

  • Access: Request and obtain a copy of your PHI.
  • Request Restrictions: Limit how your PHI is used/disclosed (with certain exceptions).
  • Request Confidential Communications: Ask that we contact you in a specific way or place.
  • Amendments: Request corrections to your PHI if it is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Accounting of Disclosures: Know when and to whom we‚Äôve disclosed your PHI.
  • File a Complaint: Contact us or the HHS Secretary (Health and Human Services) if you believe your privacy rights have been violated.

We support Covered Entities in responding to and enabling these rights.

Our Responsibilities

1. Safeguards (Required by the HIPAA Security Rule)

  • Physical Safeguards:
    • Secure office locations with controlled access.
    • Equipment containing PHI is protected with locks, alarms, and physical access logs.
  • Administrative Safeguards:
    • Annual HIPAA training for all employees and contractors.
    • Internal audits and reviews of access logs.
    • Role-based access control (minimum necessary principle).
    • Designated HIPAA Privacy & Security Officer (DPO).
  • Technical Safeguards:
    • End-to-end encryption (AES-256) for data in transit and at rest.
    • Strong authentication, including two-factor authentication (2FA) when PHI is handled.
    • Automatic logoff and inactivity timeouts.
    • Regular vulnerability scanning and penetration testing.

2. Compliance & Oversight

  • Executing a BAA with each Covered Entity and with all subcontractors who may access PHI.
  • Limiting use/disclosure of PHI to authorized purposes only.
  • Supporting Covered Entities in fulfilling individual rights (access, amendments, disclosures).
  • Performing Security Rule risk analyses and maintaining risk mitigation plans.
  • Promptly reporting any breach or security incident involving PHI.

3. Breach Notification & Mitigation

  • We immediately notify the Covered Entity and document the incident.
  • We cooperate fully to provide necessary details for accounting of disclosures.
  • We take steps to mitigate harm, including containment, root cause analysis, and process improvements.

Review & Availability

BeAware will make available to Covered Entities any internal practices, policies, procedures, or documentation required to determine compliance with HIPAA/HITECH and the applicable BAA.
